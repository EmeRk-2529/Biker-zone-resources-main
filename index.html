Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

The best thing to do is check for obvious signs that the helmet is too old. Check the manufacture date: If the manufacturer sticker says the helmet was made over five years ago, DO NOT use it. Look for visible signs of aging: If the inside or side of the helmet looks sun-faded, or brittle, it’s most likely too old to be used.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Before going on a long ride, take your bike around the block and make sure it is safe to ride. First of all, your tires are going to be deflated, it is normal after long periods of time on storage. Inflate them close to the recommended level, you can find that in the wall of the tire.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Ride with traffic, assume vehicular rules and regulations. Point in the direction one is turning. Be alert for obstacles.
